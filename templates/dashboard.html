{# ── Getting Started ── #}

Getting Started

step-by-step
This bot predicts whether BTC will go up or down in the next 5 minutes and can optionally place trades on Polymarket if you enable execution. It uses the Chainlink oracle (the same price source Polymarket uses to resolve markets) and has built-in risk rails to protect your capital. Start in paper mode first.
1
Start in Paper mode
The bot begins in PAPER mode by default. It makes predictions but does not trade real money. Watch the Signal card for a few hours to see how it performs. No wallet or funds needed.
2
Understand the Signal
The model outputs UP or DOWN with a confidence score (0.50–1.00). On this dashboard the Signal card auto-refreshes and usually updates about every ~15 seconds between retrains. Higher confidence = stronger conviction. If it's below the threshold you set in the sidebar, no trade is placed.
3
Try Dry-Run mode
Switch to DRY-RUN in the sidebar. The bot now finds real Polymarket markets and simulates trades, but never spends money. This lets you verify the full pipeline works with your wallet.
4
Go Live (small)
Set a small max $ per trade ($2–5). Switch to LIVE. The bot will place real trades on Polymarket using your connected wallet. Start small and scale up as you gain trust.
5
Monitor & Withdraw
Check the Trades & Equity tab to track wins/losses and your equity curve. When you want to take profits, use the Withdraw section in Advanced below.

{# ── v0.3.0 settings ── #}
🚀
Snipe Bet Multiplier
Multiplies your normal bet size for snipe trades only (default 2×). Because snipe accuracy is much higher, it makes sense to bet more on those trades. Still capped by your Max $/trade safety limit.
⚡
Delta-First Mode
v0.5.0 (default ON). Disables ML-only directional trades. The snipe pass at T-10s using live Chainlink delta becomes the sole entry mechanism. The ML model still trains and shows predictions on the dashboard, but doesn't trigger trades. Set C5_DELTA_FIRST=false to restore ML+snipe hybrid.
💲
Delta Pricing
v0.5.0 (default ON). Gates snipe trades based on the live ask vs. the delta magnitude. If the market asks more than the tier allows, the trade is skipped (too expensive). Small delta → cheap gate ($0.52). Large delta → permissive gate ($0.97). Tiers tunable via C5_DELTA_PRICE_T1–T5.
⏱
Trade Lead (seconds)
How many seconds before a window closes to place the trade. Default 10. Lower values = more accurate direction reading, but less time for the order to fill. Applies to snipe mode.
⏲
Cooldown (seconds)
Minimum wait time between trades. Default 300 (5 minutes = 1 window). Prevents overtrading. Set to 0 to disable (trade every window).
{# ── v0.4.0 settings ── #}
🧠
Ensemble Weight
How much the final prediction leans on the ML model vs. live Chainlink price movement. Default 0.6 = 60% model + 40% Chainlink delta. Raise to 0.8+ to rely more on the trained model; lower to 0.4 to lean more on real-time price momentum. Range: 0.0–1.0.
🌙
Quiet Hours (UTC)
Comma-separated UTC hours when the bot skips ML trades (e.g. 3,4,5 = 3:00–5:59 AM UTC). Useful for low-liquidity periods. Snipe trades are never blocked by quiet hours. Leave blank to disable.
📈
Platt Calibration auto
The model's raw probability outputs are automatically calibrated so that when it says “60%” it really means 60%. This uses Isotonic Regression on a held-out validation set. No setting to adjust — it’s always on when enough data exists (30+ training samples).
🔗
Hybrid Training Target auto
When the bot has collected enough Chainlink price history (10+ data points), it trains on Chainlink price changes instead of Coinbase. This fixes the oracle mismatch — the model now learns from the same price feed that decides winners. Activates automatically as data accumulates.
🛡
Edge Gate (Min Edge)
Filter for ML trades. Only trades when the model's edge (p − market price) exceeds this value. Default 0.03. Set to 0.02–0.05 for conservative trading. Set to 0 to disable. Snipe trades bypass this — they use their own delta threshold.
🛑
Risk Rails
Automated safety limits (circuit breakers) that pause trading when things go wrong. Protects your capital while you're away. All values adjustable in the sidebar under Risk Rails. Set any to 0 to disable that rail.
📈
Daily Loss Limit %
Pauses trading after losing this much of your balance in any 24-hour window. Default 10%. Prevents runaway losses on bad days.
🚫
Consecutive Loss Limit
Pauses trading after this many losing trades in a row. Default 5. A streak of losses may signal bad market conditions — better to pause.
🚫
Unfilled Ratio
Pauses if more than this fraction of your recent orders went unfilled. Default 0.5 (50%). High unfilled rates waste gas and signal thin liquidity.
🔎
Unfilled Lookback
How many recent orders to check for the unfilled-ratio calculation. Default 20. Smaller values react faster; larger values are more stable.
⏰
Auto-Resume (minutes)
If risk rails pause trading, it automatically resumes after this many minutes. Default 60. Set to 0 for manual-only resume (you must unpause from the dashboard).
{# ── FAQ ── #}

FAQ

common questions
How does the bot actually predict?
It uses a CNN-LSTM neural network (convolutional + recurrent) with a LightGBM/LogisticRegression fallback. It analyzes 42+ technical indicators from recent BTC candles including RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, stochastics, volume ratios, ADX, OBV, volatility regime, multi-timeframe returns, and time-of-day patterns. The model retrains every hour on fresh data to stay current.
What is Polymarket?
Polymarket is a prediction market platform where you can bet on real-world outcomes. This bot trades "SOL/BTC Up or Down in 15 minutes" markets. You buy Yes or No shares using USDC, and if you're right, each share pays out $1. If you're wrong, you lose your bet.
How much money do I need to start?
You can start with as little as $10–20 USDC. Set your max per trade to $2–5. The bot trades small amounts frequently. You don't need a big bankroll to test it out.
Can I lose money?
Yes. This is real trading. The model is not always right — a 55–60% win rate is typical. You will have losing trades. The edge comes from trading many small bets where wins slightly outnumber losses. Never bet more than you can afford to lose.
What's a "window"?
A window is a 15-minute time block (e.g. 14:00–14:15 UTC). Each window has its own Polymarket market. The bot places one trade per window. The countdown in "Live Trading" shows how much time is left in the current window.
Why does it say "Training" sometimes?
Every hour, the model retrains on the latest BTC price data (last ~8,600 candles / 30 days). This takes 30–90 seconds. During training, the signal pauses briefly. This is normal and ensures the model stays current.
What if the signal shows low confidence?
If confidence is below your threshold (set in the sidebar), the bot skips that window. No trade is placed. This is a safety feature — it only trades when it's reasonably confident. You'll see "skipped" in the trade log for those windows.
How do I know if it's actually working?
Check the Signal card — the price and direction should update frequently (usually every ~15 seconds between retrains). The "updated X ago" timestamp tells you when the last prediction was generated. In the Trades tab, you'll see logged trades with results (W/L/pending).
Why does Bet % clamp to 10% / 50% / 100%?
This is a safety rail to prevent accidental account blow-ups. By default, Bet % is capped at 10%. Enabling High-risk raises the cap to 50%. Enabling Expert/UNSAFE (typed confirmation) raises the cap to 100%.
Signal says UP/DOWN, but why did it place a different trade?
The Signal shows the model's current opinion. Trades are only attempted during the specific 5-minute window's trade time. If a trade was placed, the Signal card also shows Polymarket: order placed and the trade direction for that window. If the market wasn't available yet, confidence was below your threshold, or you were paused, it may show skipped.
What does "Reset Stats" do?
It clears all trade history, win/loss stats, and the equity chart. Useful if you've been testing and want a fresh start. This does not affect your wallet balance — it only resets the dashboard's records.
How do I withdraw profits?
Go to the Advanced section below. Enter the amount in USDC you want to withdraw, type "I understand" in the confirmation field, and submit. USDC will be sent from your trading wallet to your personal wallet address (set during setup).
What's the difference between Fixed $ and % of balance?
Fixed $: Every trade is the exact same amount (e.g. $5). Simple and predictable.
% of bal: Each trade is a percentage of your current wallet balance (e.g. 5% of $100 = $5). As you win, bets grow. As you lose, bets shrink. This is sometimes called "Kelly-style" sizing.
What is Kelly (fractional) bet sizing?
Kelly sizing increases (or decreases) your bet size based on your estimated edge. In this product, we use a simplified prediction-market version: if your model probability is $p$ and the market price is $P$, then full Kelly is: F = (p - P) / (1 - P) (only when p > P).

Because the model can be wrong, we use fractional Kelly: we multiply by Kelly fraction (default 0.25 = 1/4 Kelly). This is a practical risk control, not a profit guarantee.
Is my wallet key safe?
Your private key is stored only on your server in the config folder. It never leaves the machine. The bot uses it to sign Polymarket transactions. If you're running this on a remote server, make sure it's secured (firewall, SSH keys, strong password).
What is Snipe mode?
Snipe mode is a separate, optional trading strategy. Instead of predicting direction before the 5-minute window starts, snipe waits until the last ~10 seconds and checks whether BTC has already moved up or down compared to the window open price.

Why it works: with only 10 seconds left, the direction is mostly locked in. If BTC is already up +0.10% from the window open, it's ~85%+ likely to stay UP through resolution.

Trade-off: tokens cost more near close ($0.70–$0.95 instead of ~$0.50), so profit per trade is smaller — but accuracy is much higher. Snipe runs as a second pass and won't duplicate a trade the ML model already placed.

Enable it in the sidebar: Trading → Snipe mode.
What is Arb-first mode?
Arb-first (arbitrage-first) is a high-confidence strategy. It watches the Polymarket orderbook and tries to buy both UP and DOWN shares when the combined cost is briefly below $1.00.

Example: if UP costs $0.48 and DOWN costs $0.49, that's $0.97 total. Since one side must pay out $1.00, you lock in ~$0.03 profit per dollar spent, regardless of the outcome.

These opportunities are rare and short-lived. The bot watches for them automatically when enabled.

Enable it in the sidebar: Trading → Arb-first. The Min edge (cents) control sets how big the discount must be before trading (higher = fewer but safer trades).
What is USDC?
USDC (USD Coin) is a stablecoin cryptocurrency pegged 1:1 to the US Dollar. $5 USDC = $5 USD. It's what Polymarket uses for trading.

To fund your bot, you need to deposit USDC onto the Polygon network into your trading wallet address. You can buy USDC on exchanges like Coinbase, Kraken, or Binance, then withdraw to Polygon.
What is Polygon? What is gas / POL / MATIC?
Polygon is the blockchain network that Polymarket runs on. Think of it as the "highway" your trades travel on.

Gas is a small fee you pay to use the network (like a toll). The gas token is called POL (formerly MATIC). You need a tiny amount (~$1–$3 worth) in your wallet to pay for trade confirmations and redemptions.

If you see a "Low gas" warning, send a small amount of POL/MATIC to your bot wallet address. You can buy it on most crypto exchanges.
What is a private key? Is it safe to paste it?
A private key is like the master password to a crypto wallet. Whoever has it can spend the funds inside. That's why you should:

1. Use a dedicated bot wallet — never paste the key for your main wallet.
2. Only put small amounts ($10–50) in the bot wallet to start.
3. The key stays only on your server — it is never sent anywhere.
4. Get your Polymarket wallet key from reveal.magic.link/polymarket.
How do I fund my bot wallet with USDC?
Step 1: Find your bot wallet address (shown in the dashboard sidebar or in the Setup Wizard).
Step 2: Buy USDC on an exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, Binance, etc.).
Step 3: Withdraw USDC to your bot wallet address on the Polygon network. Make sure you select Polygon, NOT Ethereum — sending to the wrong network means lost funds.
Step 4: Also send ~$1–$3 worth of POL/MATIC (gas token) to the same address.
Step 5: The bot will see the balance automatically within a minute or two.
Docker says "permission denied" or won't start
Windows: Make sure Docker Desktop is installed and running (check the system tray for the whale icon). If it says "Docker daemon is not running", open Docker Desktop and wait for it to fully start.

Linux: You may need to add your user to the docker group: sudo usermod -aG docker $USER, then log out and back in.

Mac: Same as Windows — ensure Docker Desktop is installed from docker.com and running.
The dashboard won't load / I get "connection refused"
1. Is the container running? Check: docker ps — you should see crypto5min-polytrader in the list.
2. Is the port correct? Default is 8602. Visit http://YOUR_SERVER_IP:8602.
3. Firewall: make sure port 8602 is open on your server. On a VPS, check your hosting provider's firewall rules.
4. If using Docker Desktop locally, try http://localhost:8602.
Why does the bot skip some windows?
The bot skips a 5-minute window when:

1. Confidence is below your threshold (e.g., model is only 52% sure but threshold is 55%).
2. The cooldown hasn't expired since the last trade.
3. The market couldn't be found on Polymarket (it may not exist yet for that window).
4. Kelly sizing computed zero edge (model probability is below market price).

Skipping is normal and healthy — it means the bot is being selective. You'll see "skipped" with a reason in the trade log.
Can I run this on my laptop instead of a server?
Yes! Install Docker Desktop on Windows or Mac. The bot runs inside a container, so your laptop doesn't need Python or any special software beyond Docker.

But: if you close your laptop or it goes to sleep, the bot stops trading. For 24/7 operation, a cheap VPS ($5–10/month on Hetzner, DigitalOcean, etc.) is recommended.
{# ── v0.3.0 FAQ entries ── #}
What is the Chainlink oracle? Why does it matter?
Polymarket 15-minute markets resolve using the Chainlink price feed — a price feed from a network of independent data providers. This is different from Coinbase.

Why it matters: In older versions, the bot only watched Coinbase prices to predict direction. But Polymarket uses Chainlink to decide winner/loser. Even a $50 difference between Coinbase and Chainlink at the 5-minute boundary could flip the outcome.

What v0.3.0 does: The bot now connects to Polymarket's free WebSocket and subscribes to the exact same Chainlink price data that Polymarket uses. Snipe mode compares Chainlink open vs Chainlink current — the exact same comparison Polymarket makes. If the Chainlink feed is temporarily unavailable, it falls back to Coinbase (same as before).
What are Risk Rails?
Risk Rails are automated safety limits (circuit breakers) that pause the bot when things go wrong. They protect your capital while you're away from the dashboard.

There are four rails:
1. Daily Loss Limit — pauses after losing X% of your balance in 24 hours (default: 10%)
2. Consecutive Losses — pauses after X losing trades in a row (default: 5)
3. Unfilled Ratio — pauses if too many orders go unfilled (default: 50% of last 20 orders)
4. Auto-Resume — automatically resumes after a pause period (default: 60 minutes)

All values are adjustable in the sidebar under Risk Rails. Set any value to 0 to disable that rail. Risk state persists across restarts.
What happens when risk rails pause trading?
The bot stops placing new trades but keeps running — it still shows predictions, monitors prices, and retrains the model. Existing orders are not affected.

You'll see a "paused" status on the dashboard. Trading resumes automatically after the Auto-Resume period (default 60 minutes), or you can manually resume by clicking Resume.

Risk state is saved to logs/risk_state.json and persists across container restarts.
What is the Edge Gate?
The Edge Gate is an optional filter that blocks ML trades unless the model's probability minus the market price is above a minimum threshold.

Example: If Edge Gate = 0.03, the model predicts 58% (p=0.58), and the market asks $0.56 (P=0.56), edge = 0.02 < 0.03 → trade blocked. If the market asks $0.54, edge = 0.04 > 0.03 → trade allowed.

Snipe trades bypass the edge gate — they use their own delta-based threshold.

Set it in the sidebar under Timing & edge. Recommended: 0.02–0.05 for conservative trading. Set to 0 to disable (not recommended).
{# ── v0.4.0 FAQ entries ── #}
What is Ensemble Scoring?
In v0.4.0 the final Up/Down prediction is a blend of two signals:

1. ML Model: The trained CNN-LSTM or LightGBM model that looks at 40+ features over past candles.
2. Live Chainlink Delta: The actual price movement from the Chainlink oracle since the 5-minute window opened.

The Ensemble Weight setting controls the blend: default 0.6 means 60% model + 40% Chainlink delta. This way the bot trusts what it learned and what's happening right now.
What is Platt Calibration?
ML models often output overconfident or underconfident probabilities. Platt Calibration (actually Isotonic Regression) adjusts the model's outputs so they match real-world outcomes.

Before: Model says 55% → actual win rate might be 48%.
After: Model says 55% → actual win rate is ~55%.

This is automatic — it activates when the model has 30+ training samples. No setting to toggle.
What is the Hybrid Training Target?
Problem: The model used to train on Coinbase candle data, but Polymarket resolves markets using the Chainlink oracle — a different price feed. Small differences between them could flip the outcome.

Fix (v0.4.0): When the bot has collected enough Chainlink history (10+ data points), it switches the training target from Coinbase close to Chainlink price changes. Now the model learns to predict what Polymarket actually measures.

This activates automatically as the bot runs. In the first few hours it may still use Coinbase (not enough Chainlink data yet).
What are Quiet Hours?
Optional UTC hours when the bot skips ML trades. Some hours have low liquidity or unpredictable BTC behavior (e.g. 3–5 AM UTC).

Set in the sidebar under Timing & edge → Quiet Hours (UTC). Enter comma-separated hours: 3,4,5 means 3:00–5:59 AM UTC.

Snipe trades are never blocked by quiet hours — they only fire on strong signals. Leave blank to keep all hours active (default).
How do I update the bot?
Look at the bottom-left of the sidebar. You'll see your current version and a Check button.

Option 1 (recommended): Click the ↻ Check button. If an update is available, a green banner appears with release notes and an "Apply Update" button. Click it — the bot downloads and installs the update, then restarts automatically.

Option 2 (manual): Download the latest ZIP from the Whop product page. Stop your container, replace the files, rebuild, and restart: 
docker compose down
docker compose build --no-cache
docker compose up -d
What does "basis" mean in the trade log?
Basis is the difference between the Coinbase price and the Chainlink oracle price at the time of a trade, measured in basis points (1 bps = 0.01%).

Example: If Coinbase says $108,500 and Chainlink says $108,450, the basis is ~4.6 bps.

Small numbers are normal. Large divergences (>10 bps) could indicate pricing anomalies. This metric helps you monitor whether the two feeds are staying in sync.
